The Invasion

Argentina had claimed the Falkland Islands as Argentinian, and thus once it became permanently occupied by the BritishEmpire, relations broke down as Britain refuted the claim, and all efforts of negotiating were ceased after decades ofneglect. Argentina was controlled by a military Junta that governed both the people, and the military. The main men ofthis policy was Leopoldo Galtieri, who after years of trying to acquire the islands, negotiations broke down and theArgentine miliary invaded the islands on April 2nd, capturing the capital Port Stanley by the next day.
